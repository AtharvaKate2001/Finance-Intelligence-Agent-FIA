Investment Fundamentals

Investing involves allocating capital to assets with the expectation of generating returns over time.

Core Concepts:

1. Risk and Return:
Higher potential returns typically involve higher risk.

2. Asset Classes:
- Stocks (Equities)
- Bonds (Fixed Income)
- Real Estate
- Mutual Funds
- Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs)

3. Diversification:
Spreading investments across asset classes reduces overall portfolio risk.

4. Long-Term Perspective:
Successful investing generally requires patience and time.

5. Compound Growth:
Reinvested earnings generate additional returns over time.

Investing supports long-term wealth creation but involves inherent market risks.
