Budgeting Basics

Budgeting is the process of creating a structured plan to manage income and expenses over a specific period.

Core Principles:

1. Income Awareness:
Understanding all sources of income, including salary, business income, and passive income.

2. Expense Categorization:
Expenses are typically divided into fixed expenses (rent, utilities, loan payments) and variable expenses (entertainment, dining out).

3. The 50/30/20 Rule:
A common budgeting framework suggests allocating:
- 50% of income to needs
- 30% to wants
- 20% to savings and debt repayment

4. Emergency Fund:
Financial experts recommend maintaining an emergency fund covering 3 to 6 months of living expenses.

5. Debt Management:
Prioritizing high-interest debt repayment improves financial stability.

Budgeting helps individuals track spending, reduce financial stress, and build long-term wealth.
